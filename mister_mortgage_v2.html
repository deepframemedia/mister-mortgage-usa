Fixed Rate Mortgage

Lock in your rate today and enjoy the same predictable payment for the life of your loan — 15 or 30 years.

Stability & Peace of Mind

The Most Popular Loan in America

A Fixed Rate Mortgage offers homebuyers the ultimate peace of mind — a set interest rate and monthly payment that never changes. Whether you choose a 15-year or 30-year term, you'll always know exactly what you owe each month, making budgeting simple and stress-free.

At Mister Mortgage USA, we shop multiple lenders to secure the most competitive fixed rates available. With over 20 years of experience in South Florida, we know what it takes to get you approved quickly and at the best possible terms.

Predictable monthly payments forever

Protection from rising interest rates

15-year or 30-year term options

Ideal for long-term homeowners

Build equity consistently over time

Competitive rates for qualified buyers

Who Qualifies?

  • Credit score of 620 or higher
  • Stable employment history (2+ years)
  • Debt-to-income ratio under 45%
  • Down payment of 3%–20%
  • Primary residence, second home, or investment property
FAQ

Common Questions

What is the difference between a 15-year and 30-year fixed mortgage?+
A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but you pay far less interest and own your home sooner. A 30-year mortgage offers lower monthly payments with more flexibility, though you pay more interest overall. Both lock in your rate permanently.
Can I pay off my fixed-rate mortgage early?+
Yes! Most fixed-rate mortgages allow early payoff without penalty. Making extra principal payments reduces the life of your loan and saves you significant interest.
What credit score do I need for the best rates?+
Generally, a credit score of 740 or above qualifies you for the most competitive rates. Scores between 620-739 still qualify for fixed-rate loans, though at slightly higher rates. We work with all credit profiles.

FHA Home Loan

Government-backed financing with low down payments starting at 3.5%. Your path to homeownership starts here.

Accessible Homeownership

FHA Loans Make Homeownership Possible

The Federal Housing Administration (FHA) loan program was designed to make homeownership accessible to Americans who might not qualify for conventional financing. With a down payment as low as 3.5% and flexible credit requirements, FHA loans open the door to homeownership for millions of buyers each year.

Mister Mortgage USA is an FHA-approved lender with deep expertise in South Florida's market. We guide first-time buyers and those with less-than-perfect credit through the entire FHA process, from pre-qualification to closing.

Down payment as low as 3.5%

Flexible credit score requirements (580+)

Gift funds allowed for down payment

Competitive interest rates

Higher debt-to-income allowances

Great for first-time homebuyers

Who Qualifies?

  • Credit score 580+ for 3.5% down; 500–579 for 10% down
  • Steady employment history (2+ years preferred)
  • Property must be your primary residence
  • Property must meet FHA minimum standards
  • Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP) required
FAQ

Common Questions

What is the FHA loan limit in Miami-Dade County?+
FHA loan limits are updated annually and vary by county. In Miami-Dade County, limits are among the highest in Florida due to the area's home prices. Contact us for the current year's specific limits.
Do I have to pay mortgage insurance with an FHA loan?+
Yes, FHA loans require both an upfront Mortgage Insurance Premium (UFMIP) of 1.75% of the loan amount and an annual MIP paid monthly. This protects the lender but does add to your monthly cost.
Can I refinance out of an FHA loan later?+
Absolutely. Once you've built sufficient equity (typically 20%), you can refinance into a conventional loan to eliminate the mortgage insurance premium and potentially get a better rate.

Who Qualifies?

  • Credit score 620+ for conventional 3% down
  • Income within program limits for assistance grants

FAQ

Common Questions

What is the minimum down payment for a conventional loan?+
Conventional loans through Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac allow as little as 3% down for qualified first-time buyers and 5% for repeat buyers. Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is required until you reach 20% equity.
Are there down payment assistance grants available in Florida?+
Yes! Florida offers several programs including the Florida Assist, Florida Homeownership Loan Program (FL HLP), and various county-specific programs. Many are forgivable after a certain period. Ask us which you qualify for.
Will I pay more with a lower down payment?+
Your monthly payment will be higher due to PMI and a larger loan balance. However, staying liquid with cash reserves often outweighs the cost of PMI — especially when home values are appreciating rapidly in South Florida.

Rehab Loans (203k)

Finance your home purchase and renovation costs in one single mortgage. Turn any fixer-upper into your dream home.

Buy & Renovate

One Loan for Purchase and Renovation

The FHA 203(k) Rehabilitation Loan combines the purchase price of a home with the cost of its rehabilitation — all in one convenient mortgage. This makes it easier to buy a fixer-upper or update an existing home without taking out a separate construction or home improvement loan.

South Florida has many properties that need updating or renovation, and the 203k loan lets you buy these homes at a lower price and invest the difference into improvements that can significantly increase the property's value.

Purchase + renovation in one loan

Down payment as low as 3.5%

Covers structural & cosmetic repairs

Instant equity potential

Customize the home to your needs

Single closing saves time and money

Who Qualifies?

  • Credit score 580+ (FHA guidelines apply)
  • Minimum $5,000 in renovation costs required
  • Primary residence only
  • HUD-approved consultant required for standard 203k
  • Renovation must be completed within 6 months
FAQ

Common Questions

What types of renovations can be financed with a 203k loan?+
The Standard 203k covers major renovations including structural repairs, room additions, roofing, and HVAC. The Limited 203k (Streamline) covers cosmetic improvements like kitchens, bathrooms, flooring, and painting — up to $35,000.
How long does the 203k loan process take?+
The 203k loan process typically takes 60–90 days from application to closing due to the additional appraisal and consultant requirements. Renovation funds are released in draws as work is completed and inspected.
Do I choose my own contractor?+
Yes, you choose your own licensed, bonded contractor. The contractor must be approved by the lender and submit a detailed scope of work and cost estimate. The lender ensures funds are used appropriately.

USDA Loan

100% financing for eligible rural and suburban properties. No down payment required for qualifying borrowers.

Zero Down Payment

100% Financing in Eligible Areas

The USDA Rural Development Loan program offers 100% financing with no down payment for buyers in eligible rural and suburban areas. Backed by the U.S. Department of Agriculture, these loans offer below-market interest rates and no private mortgage insurance (PMI).

Many areas in and around South Florida qualify — more than most buyers realize. Our team can quickly determine if a property you're interested in falls within a USDA-eligible zone and whether your income meets the program requirements.

Zero down payment required

No private mortgage insurance (PMI)

Below-market interest rates

Flexible credit requirements

30-year fixed rate term

Closing costs can be rolled in

Who Qualifies?

  • Property must be in a USDA-eligible rural/suburban area
  • Household income must not exceed 115% of area median income
  • Credit score of 640+ preferred
  • U.S. citizenship or permanent residency required
  • Primary residence only — no investment properties
FAQ

Common Questions

Do I have to be a farmer to get a USDA loan?+
Absolutely not! The USDA loan program is for any homebuyer purchasing in an eligible area. The property just needs to be in a designated rural or suburban zone — not an agricultural or farming property.
How do I know if a property is USDA-eligible?+
You can check the USDA's eligibility map online, or simply share a property address with our team and we'll verify eligibility within minutes. Many suburban Florida communities qualify — including some surprising areas near urban centers.
Is there a USDA loan fee?+
Yes, USDA loans have a guarantee fee (upfront, currently 1% of the loan) and an annual fee (0.35% of the loan balance). These are much lower than FHA's MIP and can often be rolled into the loan amount.

Jumbo Loans

Tailored financing for high-value properties in South Florida's luxury real estate market.

Luxury Financing

Finance Your Dream Home

Jumbo loans are mortgages that exceed the conforming loan limits set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac — in most areas, that's anything above $766,550. In South Florida's high-demand luxury market, jumbo loans are common and require specialized expertise to navigate effectively.

At Mister Mortgage USA, we work with multiple jumbo lenders to secure the most competitive rates for high-value purchases. Whether you're buying a Miami Beach penthouse, a Coral Gables estate, or a Brickell condo, we have the connections and experience to close your deal.

Loan amounts up to $5M+

Competitive interest rates

Fixed and adjustable rate options

Primary, second home & investment

Flexible underwriting options

Personalized service from start to close

Who Qualifies?

  • Credit score typically 700+ (720+ for best rates)
  • Down payment typically 10–20%+
  • Strong income documentation required
  • Low debt-to-income ratio (under 43% preferred)
  • Cash reserves of 6–12 months of payments
FAQ

Common Questions

What is the jumbo loan limit in South Florida?+
The conforming loan limit in most Florida counties is $766,550 for 2024. Any mortgage above this amount is considered a jumbo loan and must meet different underwriting standards set by private lenders rather than Fannie/Freddie.
Are jumbo loan rates higher than conventional rates?+
Historically yes, but in recent years jumbo rates have been very competitive — sometimes even lower than conforming rates for highly qualified borrowers. The key is working with a broker who has access to multiple jumbo lenders, like Mister Mortgage USA.
Can I get a jumbo loan for an investment property?+
Yes, jumbo loans are available for primary residences, second homes, and investment properties. Requirements for investment properties are typically stricter, with higher down payment requirements and stronger financial documentation.

VA Home Loan

Exclusive homeownership benefits for veterans, active-duty service members, and surviving spouses. No down payment, no PMI.

Honoring Your Service

You Served Us — Now We Serve You

The VA Home Loan benefit is one of the most powerful homeownership tools available. Guaranteed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, VA loans offer eligible military borrowers the ability to purchase a home with zero down payment, no monthly PMI, and competitive interest rates — without the strict credit requirements of conventional loans.

At Mister Mortgage USA, we are proud to serve those who served our country. Our VA loan specialists understand the unique needs of military families and work quickly to get your pre-approval and close your loan.

Zero down payment required

No private mortgage insurance (PMI)

Competitive, below-market interest rates

Flexible credit guidelines

No prepayment penalties

Reusable benefit — use it multiple times

Who Qualifies?

  • Veterans with honorable discharge
  • Active-duty service members (90+ days service)
  • National Guard & Reserve members (6+ years)
  • Surviving spouses of fallen service members
  • Certificate of Eligibility (COE) required — we can obtain this for you
FAQ

Common Questions

Is there a VA funding fee?+
Yes, most VA borrowers pay a funding fee (typically 2.15%–3.3% of the loan for first use) which helps sustain the program. However, veterans with service-connected disabilities are exempt from this fee, and it can be rolled into the loan.
Can I use my VA loan benefit more than once?+
Yes! Your VA loan benefit is reusable. Once you've paid off your previous VA loan (or in some cases, even while keeping it), you can use the benefit again for your next home purchase.
How long does VA loan approval take?+
VA loans typically close in 40–50 days, similar to conventional loans. With an experienced VA lender like Mister Mortgage USA, we streamline the process and can often close faster by preparing your Certificate of Eligibility upfront.

What is a first-time homebuyer exactly?+

What first-time buyer programs are available in Florida?+
Florida offers several programs through the Florida Housing Finance Corporation, including the Florida Homeownership Loan Program (FL HLP), Florida Assist ($10,000 deferred loan), and various county-specific programs with grants of $5,000–$25,000.

Smart Financing for Real Estate Investors

Who Qualifies?

  • Down payment 15%–25% depending on property type

FAQ

Common Questions

What is a DSCR loan and how does it work?+
A DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) loan qualifies you based on the property's rental income rather than your personal income. If the rental income covers the mortgage payment (DSCR of 1.0+), you can qualify — making these ideal for self-employed investors or those with complex tax returns.
How much down payment is needed for an investment property?+
Conventional investment property loans typically require 15–25% down depending on the property type (single-family vs. multi-unit) and whether it's a vacation home or pure investment. DSCR and other non-QM loans may have different requirements.
Can I use rental income to qualify for the loan?+
Yes, with conventional loans you can typically use 75% of the projected rental income to offset the mortgage payment in your debt-to-income calculation. With DSCR loans, the rental income is the primary qualifying factor.

Refinancing replaces your existing mortgage with a new one — ideally at better terms. Who Qualifies?

  • Current homeowner with existing mortgage
  • Credit score 620+ (conventional) or per loan type
  • Sufficient home equity (typically 20%+ for cash-out)
  • Stable income and employment history
  • Mortgage in good standing (no recent late payments)
FAQ

Common Questions

When does it make sense to refinance?+
A refinance generally makes sense if you can lower your rate by at least 0.5–1%, if you plan to stay in the home long enough to recoup closing costs (break-even point), if you want to access equity, or if you need to change your loan terms to better fit your financial situation.
How much does it cost to refinance?+
Refinancing typically costs 2–5% of the loan amount in closing costs. However, some lenders offer no-closing-cost refinances where costs are rolled into the rate or loan balance. We'll show you all options so you can choose what makes the most financial sense.
How long does a refinance take?+
Most refinances close in 30–45 days. Streamline refinances (FHA or VA) can close faster since they require less documentation. Having your paperwork organized and responding promptly to requests helps keep the timeline on track.
